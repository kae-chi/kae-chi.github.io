We sit facing each other, I raise my line of sight to glare at you. .

My knees bent at a 24 degree angle, with my back pressed against the thick layer of glass. You sit similarly, our legs woven together in such a grotesque fashion.

The ridge of your nose, and the shape of your face are blurry. After all, I haven’t seen you in 3 years now. And for 3 years now, under the bluelight, everyone is ugly in your way.

Under the water exists a memory of us which was never conceived. Through the glass was not a school of fish, just you and me.

When I move, my head would hit the lid. You’d notice, then ask me to stay a little longer –– did that also include in my consciousness and everywhere in between?

\

We sit across from each other inside a fish tank.

I’m forced to see your shape, your fragile shape was projected, blurring my future and memories. You spoil me with your hungry, humiliating gaze scavenging everything– body, chest, legs, and face.

Leaving only my grieving, glazed eyes.
